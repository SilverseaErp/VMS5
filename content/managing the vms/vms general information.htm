The Vessel Management System (VMSV.Group Management System)

The Safety, Quality & Environmental Management System (SQEMS), referred to as the VMS for short, serves three main purposes:

  • it provides an overall introductory guide to the management system maintained by the Company
  • it states the Company's policies which govern the seafarers’ employment and social rights, the safety, health of all employees, the safe, pollution free and efficient operation of the managed ships on behalf of the Company's ship owning clients
  • it acts as basic reference for all office and shipboard manuals which control the main day to day activities undertaken by the Company and how the Company responds to unplanned events

The VMS contains procedures and instructions used by the Company to comply with the international standards relating to social and employment rights, occupational health and safety of all staff, the safe operation of ships and pollution prevention including environmental management and quality servicing , in accordance with the provisions of:

IMO Resolution A.741 (18) / ISM Code:

"International Safety Management (ISM) Code for the Safe Operation of Ships and for Pollution Prevention".

Maritime Labour Convention (MLC2006):

A joint ILO/IMO/ITF framework for seafarers’ employment and social rights for : safe and secure workplace, fair terms of employment, decent working an living conditions onboard, health protection, medical care, welfare measures and other forms of social protection

ISO 9001 Standard

A process approach used for developing, implementing and improving the effectiveness of a quality management system, to enhance customer satisfaction by meeting customer requirements.

OHSAS 18001 Standard

A system used by an organisation to develop and implement its Occupational Health and Safety (OH&S) Policy and manage its OH&S risks

ISO 14001 Standard

A system enabling an organisation to establish, assess the effectiveness of, and conform to, procedures to set an environmental policy and objectives.

ISO 50001ISO 50001:2011 Energy management systems – Requirements with guidance for use is a specification created by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) for an energy management system. The standard specifies the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining and improving an energy management system, whose purpose is to enable an organization to follow a systematic approach in achieving continual improvement of energy performance, including energy efficiency, energy security, energy use and consumption. The standard aims to help organizations continually reduce their energy use, and therefore their energy costs and their greenhouse gas emissions.:2011 Standard

A system which enables organizations to establish the systems and processes necessary to improve energy performance, including energy efficiency, use and consumption.

This Company VMS describes how the Company's policy is implemented and represents a reference guide to all personnel involved in the Company's activities.

The policies and procedures outlined herein are to be adhered to by all office-based staff and all seagoing personnel.

This VMS is the property of the Company and its use is restricted to the Company itself and cannot be distributed to anybody without prior authorisation of the Senior Vice President - Marine Operations(except as per section below 7 - Document Modification and Document Control – Company Management Cells and third parties)

The VMS is freely accessible for consultation to all staff members. The intention of the text in the VMS is to be gender-less. Where male gender (ie “he” or “his”) is only used it is to be regarded equally applicable for the female gender.

1 - Definitions
Administration The Government of the State whose flagThe flag state of a commercial vessel is the state under whose laws the vessel is registered or licensed. The flag state has the authority and responsibility to enforce regulations over vessels registered under its flag, including those relating to inspection, certification, and issuance of safety and pollution prevention documents. As a ship operates under the laws of its flag state, these laws are used if the ship is involved in an admiralty case. The term "flag of convenience" describes the business practice of registering a merchant ship in a sovereign state different from that of the ship's owners, and flying that state's civil ensign on the ship. Ships are registered under flags of convenience to reduce operating costs or avoid the regulations of the owner's country. Since the 1921 Flag Right Declaration, it has been recognised that all states—including land-locked countries—have a right to be a flag state. the ship is entitled to fly.

2 - Application and Scope

The VMS requirements are applicable to the following services performed within the Company:

  • Technical Operations including Purchasing
  • Marine Operations including Port and Itinerary Planning
  • Safety, Quality, Security and Environmental Protection / Marine Compliance
  • Crewing
  • Insurance
  • Accounting & Administration / Finance
  • Hotel Operations and Passengers Services

Scope for ISO9001/14001/500001 and ISO45001:

The Scope of the Management System for the purpose of the above Standards is:

“Management of (Passenger) Ships”

Exclusions under ISO9001

The services provided by the Company to the client are controlled by the Management Agreement in place. The Company is not contracted to provide a service relating to section 7.3 of the Standard on “Design and Development,” and therefore this is not included in this Section.

3 - Manual Revisions

The VMS is reviewed and updated on a regular basis (typically every quarter) under the responsibility and approval of the Marine Manager and for global and policy changes also by the Senior Vice President - Marine Operations

Any paper copies printed out from the e-version may be used for reference only and will bear a statement in the footer “Printed copies are uncontrolled documents and for reference only. Always refer to the electronic controlled copy for the latest version”

5 - Management System Development

The VMS is controlled by MOCD and is issued to new vessels entering management by means of a CD along with certain paper-copy records and log books.

The VMS will be maintained through the VMS Platform’s direct replication between the vessel and MOCD. To avoid numerous changes being streamed to the vessel, updates will be scheduled on a quarterly basis with such changes being based on procedure change requests and Masters’ reviews. Any safety-critical changes will be published without delay.

Where it is necessary to issue significant changes to the VMS, such as updating reference documents, where the file size prohibits shore-ship replication, the VMS will be re-issued to all vessels by way of a CD and the VMS Revision number will be changed accordingly.

All new procedures and revisions are to be checked and approved by the MOCD Heads and the MOCD Manager. All original master documents and records of revision are to be retained by MOCD.

Plans, procedures and instructions for key shipboard operations concerning the safety of the ship, passengers, crew and for preventing pollution are to be developed by ship and shore staff in compliance with the following:

  • Entry into force of Classification Society, National or International rules and regulations.
  • The recommendations of :
    • Ship’s Captains and Senior Officers
    • Shore Heads of Department, Superintendents. Managers, etc.
    • Ships’ Safety, Health Hygiene & Environment Protection Committee
    • Management Reviews Committee,.
  • Reports and statistics arising from non conformities and hazardous occurrences.
  • The recommendations of Insurers, Charterers, the Classification Society and the maritime industry.

These procedures and instructions will assign duties and responsibilities to qualified personnel in accordance with the principles of the VMS and Company policy.

All office & shipboard staff may present recommendations for improvement of the Management System to their respective Heads of Department.

Office Personnel will send the form SAF85 (VMS Procedure Change Request (PCR) to the Manager (Management Systems)

Masters will submit their recommendations and suggestions through the “Master’s S&QMS Review” SAF69 form via the respective DPA who will forward to the Manager (Management Systems).

The Marine Manager (MS) after consultation with Management will advise the originator (if Office Personnel) of the PCR or the Master through the relevant DPA, if the change request has been accepted or not by submitting back the completed copy of the SAF85 with the relevant details contained therein.

The Marine SQ Department is responsible for updating and distributing the documentation.

The Senior Vice President - Marine Operationswill approve amendments to the Company and its Policies Section, prior to issue.

The Marine Manager will approve amendments to the Operating procedures and forms prior to issue.

Modifications to the Operating Procedures and associated forms will be discussed and coordinated with the department heads in the office, as applicable:

  • Head of Leisure HSEQ for marine, safety & environmental protection, medical, administration matters.
  • Technical Director for technical matters.
  • Crew Director for personnel matters.
  • Director Hotel Operations & Passenger Services and Marine Manager for hygiene and sanitation matters.
  • Financial Controller for finance & office accounting matters.
  • Director Hotel Operations & Passenger Services for hotel operations, hotel purchasing, hotel administration, purser, housekeeping and food & beverage matters.

The release of such amended documentation will be authorised by the Marine Manager (Management Systems).

6 - Document Modification and Document Control

Management System Controlled Documents are identified with a Revision number/date and forms also additionally with a Version number/date.

To aid identification of the latest revision only, revised text in the procedures and forms is highlighted in yellow.

For Forms Versions are major changes to a controlled document associated with re-structuring of paragraphs.

In order to identify the current revision status of controlled documents, a Master List Document Matrix is maintained.

The controlled and official version of the Management System / VMS is distributed via a CD in an electronic version.

Ships

The electronic version of the Management System is to be placed on a networked computer onboard so that it is available throughout the vessel and protected against modification/deletion.

The Master shall keep under his custody the latest source CD with the Management System.

Copying and printing of relevant sections of the Management System is permitted for reference and operational purposes only (ie navigation procedures on the bridge) but such copies and printouts are to be considered “Uncontrolled Documents”.

When printing out extracts of the Management System a watermark will appear “Uncontrolled Document”- “(refer to the electronic controlled copy for the latest version)”.

Most of the Tier III documents above (such as ship specific manuals including the Emergency Contingency Plan (ECP) and Master’s Decision Support System, SOPEP, etc) will retain their hard copies as the document controlled versions.

Company Management Cells and third parties

Company Management Cells will also receive a copy of the latest revision of the VMS. They shall manage adequately obsolete copies

The whole VMS or parts thereof (incl. relevant forms) will be distributed, as applicable, upon each revision, to the Company approved Crewing Agents (MLC SPRS) and other third parties, such as clients (ship Owners/Operators))

Document and Data Changes

On receipt of a new revision of the Management System CD, both all the old controlled e-copies and all the old uncontrolled printed parts of the Management System shall be deleted/destroyed and replaced with the latest revisions

Paper Tier III Manual holders are to insert amendments, destroy the old documentation and confirm receipt of the amendments.

All Officers (including Master and Ch. Engineer) shall review and sign for acknowledgement of familiarization upon receipt of a new revision of the Management CD, by printing out the acknowledgement sheets and storing them in hard copy onboard till receipt of a next revision as follows:

  • The Company & its Policies – All Officers
  • Fleet Operations Procedures - Deck Operations - by All Deck Officers
  • Fleet Operations Procedures - Technical Operations– by all Technical (engine and electrical departments) Officers
  • Safety and Environmental Protection- All Officers
  • Medical – by the Medical Department and Senior Deck Officers (Captain, Staff Captain, Safety Officer and Environmental Officer if available)
  • Fleet Operations Procedures – Technical Administration – All Deck and Technical Officers and Hotel Senior Onboard Management
  • Personnel- All Officers
  • Hygiene & Sanitation – all Hotel Officers and Senior Deck (Captain, Staff Captain, Safety Officer and Environmental Officer if available) and Senior Technical Officers (Chief Engineer, Staff Engineer, Hotel Engineer, Chief Electrician)
  • Hotel Operations – where the Company does hotel management – by Hotel Officers, as applicable

Heads of Department onboard may assign additional officers and staff to review and acknowledgment familiarization with the above Manuals, as applicable.

Documents of External Origin

Documents of external origin maintained in the office or onboard the vessel (e.g. Flag State legislation, Class Society and IMO publications) are controlled by maintenance of form SAF 8 “Onboard Publications List”.

Other documents of external documents are Class Rules, ship drawings, equipment manuals, clients’ requirements / procedures, etc.

The Master shall designate an officer to track the upgrading/amending of such documents with the amendments received by the issuing authority and ensure that the correct version or issue is available onboard.

7 - Process Control

The Company identifies certain processes where monitoring, measurement and analysis can be performed to ensure effective control.

These processes are termed “key performance indicators” (KPI), and are subject to review to ensure that the planned results are being achieved.

Each Departmental Head, if relevant, will identify KPI’s and have responsibility for monitoring their progress. KPI’s are listed on the Annual Management Review Report.
