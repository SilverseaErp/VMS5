Regional Management Review of the VMSV.Group Management System

1 - Scope

This section details Company guidelines and procedures for Management System Review with the purpose to:

  • assess how the system is operating
  • identify areas for improvement
  • ensure uniformity of shipboard reviews and cohesive feedback to the Company Management Review

Management System Review is a requirement of the ISM Code, ISO 9001:2008, ISO 14001:2004 and ISO45001, which form an integral part of the Company’s Management System

2 - Responsibilities

1.1 Shore

Senior Vice President - Marine Operations
  • Responsible for the “Office management review procedure”
Fleet Management Cell
  • Responsible for the “Shipboard management review procedure”

Only for vessels certified to one of the Standards ISO14001ISO 14000 is a family of standards related to environmental management that exists to help organizations (a) minimize how their operations (processes, etc.) negatively affect the environment (i.e., cause adverse changes to air, water, or land); (b) comply with applicable laws, regulations, and other environmentally oriented requirements, and (c) continually improve in the above. and/or ISO45001

Marine S&Q Manager
  • Responsible for collating data for discussion and preparation of the draft and final Annual Management Review report

1.2 Shipboard

Shipboard Management
  • Responsible for the “Shipboard management review procedure”

Only for vessels certified to one of the Standards ISO14001 and/or ISO45001

3 - Procedures

Office management review procedure

The Senior Vice President - Marine Operations does the procedure:

1) Hold Management Review Meeting annually in the office, with the following objective:

“Review and evaluation of the effectiveness of the Management System ashore and onboard ships”

The Management System review should be for the past year, and usually takes place within the first quarter of the new year

a) include review of Company Policies

b) if deemed necessary, convene further meetings to maintain and improve the effectiveness of the Management System

2) Chair the meeting, and:

a) ensure participation of Company Senior Management, including:

- office Heads of Departments

- Fleet Managers

- Marine Managers

- Managing Cells Directors

3) Discuss the data in the drafted Management Review report, using the following agenda:

The Marine S&Q Department will collate and prepare data for discussion, produce draft review report and co-ordinate any corrective actions identified

a) review of last Annual Management Review’s Minutes

b) vessels under management

c) office certifications

d) office audits:

- external and internal

- findings and analysis

e) shipboard audits:

- external and internal

- findings and analysis

f) external shipboard inspection:

- Port StatePort State Control (PSC) is the inspection of foreign ships in other national ports by PSC officers (inspectors) for the purpose of verifying that the competency of the master and officers on board, and the condition of the ship and its equipment comply with the requirements of international conventions (e.g. SOLAS, MARPOL, STCW, etc.) and that the vessel is manned and operated in compliance with applicable international law. Control

- FlagThe flag state of a commercial vessel is the state under whose laws the vessel is registered or licensed. The flag state has the authority and responsibility to enforce regulations over vessels registered under its flag, including those relating to inspection, certification, and issuance of safety and pollution prevention documents. As a ship operates under the laws of its flag state, these laws are used if the ship is involved in an admiralty case. The term "flag of convenience" describes the business practice of registering a merchant ship in a sovereign state different from that of the ship's owners, and flying that state's civil ensign on the ship. Ships are registered under flags of convenience to reduce operating costs or avoid the regulations of the owner's country. Since the 1921 Flag Right Declaration, it has been recognised that all states—including land-locked countries—have a right to be a flag state.

h) insurance status and claims

i) review of the effectiveness of the Management System:

a) corrective action with time frame

Details will be given in appendices to the final Review report

6) Review the final Management Review report

7) Approve circulation to each:

a) office department

b) vessel

 

Shipboard management review procedure

The assigned Fleet Management Cell and Shipboard Command of vessels certified to one of the Standards ISO14001 and/or ISO45001 do the procedure:

1) Hold annually Management review joint meetings, using form SAF110 “Shipboard Management Review (annually)”

2) Pass the completed form SAF110 to the Marine S&Q department after each management review

The outcome of these annual shipboard management reviews on ISO14001/ISO45001 will supplement the Office Annual Management Review
