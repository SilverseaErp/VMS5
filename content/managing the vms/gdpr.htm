Personal Data Protection (GDPR)

1 - Introduction

1.1 Objective

This section, together with other referenced materials documents the Company’s policies and procedures on personal data protection, to comply with relevant requirements such as:

  • Regulation (EU) 2016/679 – on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data
    • known as GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) in effect as of 25th May 2018
  • Other States’ specific data protection regulations, such as
    • USA (with sector specific federal laws and individual states specific laws
    • Russia (Data Protection Act 2006)
    • Ukraine (Data Protection Law 2010)
    • Philippines (Data Privacy Act 2012)
  • Personal Data does not affect only Information Technology (IT) but any other files and records of personal information

This section covers only the activities of the Company (defined under the ISM Code as per section VMS) associated with the management of ships.

1.2 Application and penalties

The GDPR applies to:

  • all organizations that collect or process data of European Union (EU) or European Economic Area (EEA) residents or of persons based in EU /EEA
  • therefore applies also to organizations based outside EU/EEA
  • data that contains personal information (personal data) only

GDPR Infringement fines can be up to EUR 20 million or up to 4% of the worldwide annual turnover whichever is greater

1.3 Definitions

  • Personal Data- any information relating to an identified or identifiable person (“Data subject”)
    • Example-
    • Onboard and Shore - Crew or shore employees’ data such as personal documents, contracts, employment agreements, certificates, medical certificates, CVs etc
    • Onboard or Ashore - Medical history logs/records/ reports
    • Onboard or Ashore - Security data: visitors lists, personal IDs information
    • Onboard or Ashore - Hazardous occurrences (personal accidents) reports
    • Onboard - Public health data - Acute Gastroenteritis logs, symptomatic / isolated persons data
    • Onboard - Crew and Passenger Manifests /lists
    • Onboard and Ashore – passengers personal information provided during cruise booking, ticketing etc
  • Data subject - a person who can be identified from the data (directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier or to one or more factors specific to his/her physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity)
    • Example- you, a colleague, a client, a crew member, a passenger, a contractor
  • Processing - any operation which is performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure or destruction
  • Controller - the person or organization who determines how to use the data (the purposes and means of the processing of personal data)
    • Example – the Company or a business unit / department – Human Resources , Crewing Office etc both ashore and onboard
  • Processor - the person or organisaton who processes personal data on behalf of the controller
    • Example – an employee (or a crew member) within the business unit / department or a 3rd party (e.g. a recruitment agent, ship agent etc)
  • Recipient – a person or organisation, to which the personal data are disclosed, whether a third party or not. (public authorities which may receive personal data in the framework of particular enquiry shall not be regarded as recipients
  • Third Party – a person or organisation other than the data subject, controller, processor and persons who, under the direct authority of the controller or processor, are authorised to process personal data;
  • Consent of the data subject – any freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes by which he or she, by a statement or by a clear affirmative action, signifies agreement to the processing of personal data relating to him or her;
  • Personal Data Breach – breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data transmitted, stored or otherwise processed
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO) – the employee assigned by the Company as responsible for monitoring compliance and providing information / advice on data protection law
    • An employee with professional experience and knowledge of data protection law, who reports directly to Company’s top management
  • Supervisory Authority – the government body responsible for enforcing data protection law
    • Example – an Information Commissioner’s Office in some countries

1.4 Main Principles for Personal Data

  • lawfulness, fairness and transparency - process lawfully, fairly and in a transparent manner
  • purpose limitation – collect for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes and do not further process in a manner that is incompatible with those purposes;
  • data minimization - adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed
  • accuracy - accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date;
    • take every reasonable step to ensure that personal data that are inaccurate, are erased or rectified without delay
  • storage limitation - keep in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary
  • integrity and confidentiality - process in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the personal data, including protection against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction or damage
  • accountability – the controller is accountable in case of a breach

1.5 Main requirements

  • Apply the principle above
  • Have the legal grounds for processing personal data
  • Respect individual/personal rights of data subjects
2 - Procedures

Personal Data Compliance Procedure

2.1 Persons who are Data Subjects

You have the right to:

1) know why your data is required and how it will be used

2) access your personal data to verify lawfulness of processing

3) have your personal data rectified if it is inaccurate or incomplete for the purpose

4) have your data erased in certain circumstances / “be forgotten”

5) block or stop processing of your personal data

6) transfer your personal data to another controller for your purposes

7) object to the processing of your personal data

8) not having decisions made about you solely by automated means, including profiling

2.2 Company’s Top Management:

1) Stipulate Personal Data Policy on

a) Data protection

b) Personal Data Incident notification

c) Data Retention and destruction

d) Data Transfers

e) Privacy

2) Communicate the policies to all staff (e.g. make them accessible on intranet website), including third party processors

3) Establish and implement the policies with relevant procedures

4) Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO)

5) Conduct periodically personal data compliance audits

2.3 Data Collectors – Company and relevant departments - ashore and onboard

1) Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to the personal data:

a) amount collected,

b) the extent of processing,

c) the period of storage

d) accessibility

2) Collect and process personal data fairly, lawfully and transparently

3) Collect and process personal data for specific and legitimate purposes

4) Collect only adequate and limited data to what is necessary only for processing

5) Maintain personal data accurate and up-to-date for the purpose

6) Rectify or delete inaccurate or obsolete data

7) Keep personal data secure and confidential only to the ones who need to know

8) Keep data only as long as necessary for it to be processed

9) Demonstrate compliance with the principle above

10) Instruct Data Processors accordingly as above

11) Ensure Processor has implemented compliant measures

2.4 Processors – employees within Company departments ashore and on board and third parties agents who handle personal data

1) Follow the requirements of the Company / your Data Controller

2) Be familiar with the GDPR

3) Understand how it applies to your duties and responsibilities

4) Respect the individual rights of each person / data subject as above

5) Apply the principles of data protection above

6) Demonstrate compliance

7) Report any (suspected) potential data breach as a Personal Data Incident to the Data Protection Officer (DPO), as soon as possible

 

Do not automatically assume that there has been a Personal Data Breach. Always use the term “Personal Data Incident”.

The DPO will make final determination.

The GDPR has a deadline of reporting of personal data breaches within 72 hrs – so prompt reporting to your DPO is essential

2.5 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

1) Monitor compliance

2) Provide guidance as required

3) Follow up and analyze any “Personal Data Incidents” reported to determine whether or not are “Personal Data Breach”

4) Report any Personal Data Breaches to Company’s top management and to the relevant Supervisory authorities within 72 hours

5) Investigate Personal Data Breaches and implement measures to prevent recurrence
