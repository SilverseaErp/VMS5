Document Control

1 - Internally controlled documents

The following are internally controlled documents:

  • VMSV.Group Management System Procedures and Forms (MS&Q)
  • Work Instructions (MS&Q)
  • VSL Bulletins (MS&Q)
  • Fleet Letters (Fleet Assistant)
  • Environmental Management Team documentation (MS&Q)
  • Energy Management Documentation (MS&Q)
  • Training Material (MS&Q)
2 - Externally controlled documents

Are documents prepared by external parties such as:

  • Classification Society Rules
  • British Standards
  • Ship’s Drawings
  • Equipment Instruction Manuals

They are to be held and registered in a library within the office.

In the event that an up-to-date copy of a drawing is required, it is to be requested from the shipbuilder/repairer/owner as appropriate. It is the responsibility of the user of an externally controlled document such as this, to ensure that the correct issue or version is being used.

The Fleet Director or his nominee is to be responsible for:

  • The establishment of an Index or Master List of Externally Controlled Documents and their status.
  • The updating/amending of the documents concerned, with the amendments received from the issuing authority.
  • The destroying of obsolete documents.
3 - Uncontrolled documentation

Uncontrolled documents are, but not limited to:

  • Hard copies of the VMS procedures for personal use or reference only.
  • Documents other than those described above.
4 - Changes to Controlled Documentation

Changes to controlled documents are to be governed by the same authorisation and issue procedures applied to the original document.

The recipient of an amendment to a controlled document is responsible for inserting the amendment in the applicable document.

All superseded VMS and other documents (both office and shipboard) are to be destroyed.

All other superseded documents are to be marked as such and the person making the change is to initial and date the document and add a note stating the new document issue.

Uncontrolled when printed
