Deviation to Company Instructions

In certain circumstances there are particular requirements requested by owners which may differ from the Company standard instructions / procedures but are acceptable to the Company as the procedure to be followed for that owner only. In such cases, a form “Deviation to Company Instructions” (Form RSQ 09) is to be completed.

The Senior Vice President - Marine Operations or his nominee is responsible and accountable for:

  • Authorising deviations to company instructions by signature
  • Issuing of deviations to company instructions
  • Maintaining a file of deviations to company instructions
  • Maintaining a register of deviations to company instructions
  • Withdrawing deviations to company instructions

Distribution of the Deviations to Company Instructions Form, is to be as follows:

  • Originals - Office File - Deviations to Company Instructions
  • Copy - To the ship to be entered into the relevant VMSV.Group Management System section
  • Copy - To the ship for entry into the Master’s Handover File
  • Copy - To MOCD

Shipboard

  • Shipboard non-conformity reports will document deviations from the Management System regarding:
    • Occupational Health, Safety, Pollution prevention and Environmental protection matters.

    These shall be reported to the DPADesignated Person Ashore with copy to the Marine Manager (MS).

    • The Quality System and its documentation.

These shall be reported to the Quality Management Representative (Marine Manager (MS))

  • Such shipboard non-conformities may be raised as follows:
    • During internal shipboard audits by the SMS Form SAF23A “Audit Finding Report”
    • During Master’s review of the Management System (SAF69)
    • As required by shipboard staff (authorised by the Master) against the Company’s SMS via the SMS Foms SAF23B” Shipboard Raised Non Conformance Report”
  • Shipboard non-conformity reports should describe the non-conformity and the corrective action agreed between the Master / Office HoD (as applicable) and the DPA.
  • The DPA / Management Representative (as applicable) shall:
    • Discuss any non-conformity raised with the relevant parties and, where appropriate, the Head of Department in the office in order to confirm that the corrective action is appropriate.
    • Verify that corrective actions are implemented.
    • Ensure it is entered for tracking in the Shipsure software database.
  • The Master is responsible for ensuring that the shipboard corrective action is implemented by relevant shipboard Head of Department.
